### DESIGN RULES

1. **One idea per slide** - No walls of text
2. **Maximum 6 bullet points per slide** - Prefer 3-4
3. **Large font for main points** - 28pt minimum for body, 40pt+ for titles
4. **Generous whitespace** - Let content breathe
5. **Consistent alignment** - Left-align text, center titles
6. **Progressive disclosure** - Simple in main deck, detailed in appendix
7. **No animations or transitions** - Keep it professional and clean
8. **Slide numbers** - Bottom right, small and subtle

### TONE & MESSAGING

- **Confident but not arrogant** - "I've been building this" not "I'm the best"
- **Vision-focused** - Paint the future, don't sell
- **Enterprise-appropriate** - Professional, not startup-y buzzwords
- **Conversational language** - Written to be spoken, not read
- **Strategic framing** - Connect everything to Amex value

### KEY PHRASES TO INCLUDE

- "What if every employee had their own AI team?"
- "Specialized agents that know how Amex works"
- "On-premise, no external calls, audit everything"
- "Constrained to approved knowledge - can't make things up"
- "Scale AI adoption across 60,000+ employees without chaos"
- "Not a concept - it's a working system"

### KEY PHRASES TO AVOID

- "Hire me" or "I'm the perfect candidate"
- "Game-changing" or "Revolutionary" (overused)
- "AI-powered" as buzzword (be specific)
- Any specific timelines in exact weeks/months (say "phased approach")
- Criticism of current Amex tools or processes
